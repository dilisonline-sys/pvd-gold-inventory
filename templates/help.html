1. Overview

The PVD Goldsmith Manufacturing System is a web-based platform for managing the complete lifecycle of jewelry production — from customer order to final delivery. It covers:

  • 11-stage manufacturing pipeline with automatic date/time tracking at each stage
  • Inventory management for raw materials (gold, silver, gems, chemicals)
  • Customer orders with revenue tracking
  • Quality control checks per production stage
  • Reports for production efficiency, gold consumption, and worker productivity
  • Role-based access so each team member sees only what they need
Tip: The sidebar on the left gives you access to every section. Your role determines which sections are visible to you.

3. User Roles & Permissions

Every user is assigned one of six roles. The system automatically restricts menu items and actions based on the logged-in user's role.

Administrator

Full access to everything in the system.

  • Create, edit, and deactivate user accounts
  • Access all manufacturing, inventory, order, and report features
  • Access the Django system admin panel (/admin/)
  • Change any user's role
Manager

All operational features plus all reports. Cannot manage user accounts.

  • View and manage all production jobs and orders
  • Access production, inventory, gold consumption, and worker reports
  • Approve or reject material issuances
Supervisor

Create and manage jobs and orders; manage stage operations.

  • Create new production jobs and assign workers to stages
  • Advance jobs through the production pipeline
  • Issue materials and record QC checks
  • Cannot access management reports
Production Worker

Update stages they are personally assigned to.

  • View the job list and job details
  • Update weight, notes, and status for their assigned stage only
  • Cannot create jobs, advance stages, or view reports
Inventory Clerk

Full inventory management access.

  • Add and edit raw materials and suppliers
  • Record stock entries (individual and bulk CSV upload)
  • View stock history and low-stock alerts
  • Cannot access manufacturing or order management
Viewer

Read-only access across all modules. Can view but not edit anything.

5. Manufacturing
The 11-Stage Production Pipeline

Every production job moves through the following stages in order. Each stage is linked automatically to the next:

Design→ Wax Carving→ Investment / Burnout→ Casting→ Filing→ Polishing→ Stone Setting→ Quality Control→ Plating→ Final Inspection→ Packaging

A job can only be at one stage at a time. Advancing creates a new process record for the next stage automatically.

Creating a Production Job

Who can do this: Admin Manager Supervisor

1
In the sidebar, click Manufacturing → Job List.
2
Click the New Job button (top-right of the Job List page).
3
Fill in the job form:
  • Job Order — link to an existing customer order (optional)
  • Description — e.g. "18K Gold Ring with Diamond"
  • Metal Type — Gold, Silver, Platinum, etc.
  • Purity — e.g. 18K, 22K, 925
  • Gross Weight (g) — starting weight of the piece
  • Priority — Low / Medium / High / Urgent
  • Target Completion Date — when the job should be finished
  • Special Instructions — any notes for the production team
4
Click Save. The system automatically assigns a job number (format: PJ-YYYY-NNNN) and the current date/time as the creation timestamp. The job starts at the Design stage.
Example
Creating a job with Description "22K Bangle — Fatima Wedding Set", Metal Type Gold, Purity 22K, Gross Weight 48.5g, Priority High, Target Date 15 Jun 2026 will generate job number PJ-2026-0001 automatically.
Updating a Stage

Who can do this: Supervisor Manager Admin — and Workers for their assigned stages.

1
Open a job from the Job List by clicking its job number.
2
Click Update Stage (gold button, top of the page).
3
Set the Status:
  • In Progress — the Started At date/time is recorded automatically right now.
  • Completed — the Completed At date/time is recorded automatically right now.
  • Pending / On Hold / Failed — no auto-timestamp.
4
Optionally override Started At or Completed At if the work actually happened at a different time (e.g., recording yesterday's work today).
5
Enter Weight In and Weight Out (grams). The system calculates the material loss automatically.
6
Click Save Stage Update. To move to the next stage, use the Advance to Next Stage button on the job detail page instead.
Example — Casting Stage
Polisher sets Status to In Progress. System records Started At: 28 May 2026, 09:15 automatically. After casting is done, they set Status to Completed, enter Weight In 52.3g, Weight Out 50.8g. System records Completed At: 28 May 2026, 14:30 and calculates loss of 1.5g.
Automatic Date & Time Tracking

The system records dates and times at every key event — you do not need to type them in:

EventWhat is recorded automatically
Job createdCreation date & time (shown in the job list)
Stage status → In ProgressStarted At timestamp on the process record
Stage status → CompletedCompleted At timestamp on the process record
Stage advanced to nextCurrent stage auto-completed with timestamp; new stage record created with its own timestamp
QC check recordedQC check date & time (Checked At)
Material issuedIssuance date & time (Issued At)
Stock entry recordedEntry date & time
Order placedOrder creation date & time

The Process Records table on every job detail page shows all stages sorted by most recent first, with Started At and Completed At columns visible.

All timestamps are saved in your server's timezone. If your server is hosted remotely, confirm the timezone is set correctly in Django settings (TIME_ZONE).
Quality Control (QC) Checks

Who can do this: Supervisor Manager Admin

1
Open a job and click QC Check (blue button).
2
Select the Stage being checked and choose a Result: Pass, Fail, or Conditional.
3
Enter Defects Found and any Corrective Actions required.
4
Save. The check date and time are recorded automatically. All QC records appear in the job detail page.
Example
After the Polishing stage, QC Inspector records: Result Conditional, Defects "Minor scratch on inner band", Corrective Action "Re-polish inner surface for 10 minutes". The QC record is timestamped immediately.
6. Inventory Management

The inventory module tracks all raw materials used in production — gold alloys, silver, gemstones, chemicals, and tools.

Recording a Stock Entry

Who can do this: Inventory Clerk Supervisor Manager Admin

1
Click Inventory → Stock Entry in the sidebar.
2
Select the Material from the dropdown (e.g. "Gold 18K Alloy").
3
Enter the Quantity (in the material's unit — grams, pieces, litres) and the Unit Cost. The total cost is calculated automatically.
4
Optionally fill in Supplier, Batch Number, Purity, and Notes.
5
Click Save Stock Entry. The entry date and time are recorded automatically. The current stock balance updates instantly.
Example
Receiving a delivery: Material Gold 18K Alloy, Quantity 250g, Unit Cost AED 85.00/g, Supplier Gulf Gold Suppliers LLC, Batch GG-2026-047. System records Total Cost AED 21,250.00 and timestamps the entry at the current date/time.
Bulk Stock Upload via CSV

Use this when you receive a large delivery with many different materials at once.

1
Click Inventory → Bulk Stock Upload.
2
Click Download Sample CSV to get a template file.
3
Open the CSV in Excel or any spreadsheet app. Fill in your data — one row per material.
4
Save the file as .csv format and upload it on the Bulk Stock Upload page.
5
The system processes each row. Rows with errors are reported individually — valid rows are still saved.
CSV Format
material_name,quantity,unit_cost,supplier_name,batch_number,purity,notes Gold 18K Alloy,250.5,85.00,Gulf Gold Suppliers,GG-047,18K,Yellow gold alloy ingot Silver 925,1200.0,1.20,Silver House FZE,SH-2026-12,925,Sterling silver wire Diamond Round 0.5ct,50,320.00,Gem Traders LLC,GT-D-099,,Round brilliant cut Casting Wax,200,0.45,Industrial Supply,,,Red injection wax
Columns: material_name (required), quantity (required), unit_cost (required), supplier_name, batch_number, purity, notes
Note: The material_name must exactly match a material already in the system. If the material does not exist, that row will be skipped with an error message. Add the material first via Inventory → Raw Materials → Add Material.
Low Stock Alerts

The system automatically flags materials when their stock falls at or below the Minimum Stock Level set on each material.

  • View all alerts at Inventory → Low Stock Alerts
  • The dashboard also shows a summary count in the stat cards
  • To update the minimum level, go to Inventory → Raw Materials and edit the material
Example
If Gold 18K Alloy has Minimum Stock Level = 100g and current stock = 85g, it appears in the Low Stock Alerts list highlighted in red.
7. Customer Orders
Creating an Order

Who can do this: Supervisor Manager Admin

1
Click Orders → Customers. Add the customer if they are new (Add Customer button).
2
Click Orders → Job Orders → New Order.
3
Select the Customer, enter Item Type (Ring, Necklace, Bracelet, etc.), and fill in design details, metal type, stone specifications, and pricing.
4
Set the Required Date — the customer's deadline. The system will flag the order as overdue if this date passes before delivery.
5
Click Save. An order number is generated automatically (format: JO-YYYY-NNNN) and the order date/time is recorded.
Example
Customer Mrs. Al-Rashidi, Item Type Ring, Metal Gold 18K, Stone Ruby 2ct centre, 10 diamond accents, Total Price AED 4,800, Advance Paid AED 2,000, Required Date 20 Jun 2026. System generates order JO-2026-0012 with Balance Due = AED 2,800.
Order Status Flow
StatusMeaning
PendingOrder received; not yet in production
In ProductionLinked production job is active
On HoldPaused — waiting for customer confirmation, stone delivery, etc.
CompletedItem delivered to customer
CancelledOrder cancelled
Use the Add Note button on the order detail page to record customer communications, design changes, or deposit payments — each note is timestamped automatically.
8. Reports

Who can access reports: Admin Manager

All reports are accessed from the Reports section in the sidebar.

ReportWhat it showsUse case
Production Report Jobs by status, average completion time, jobs per stage Monitor production throughput and identify bottlenecks
Inventory Report Current stock levels, total stock value, stock by category Assess raw material stock value and coverage
Gold Consumption Weight issued vs. weight recovered per job and per stage; waste percentage Track gold losses, identify wasteful stages, audit material usage
Order Report Orders by status, revenue, overdue count, delivery performance Track revenue and monitor on-time delivery
Worker Productivity Jobs completed per worker, average stage duration Evaluate team performance and plan staffing
Example — Gold Consumption
The Gold Consumption report for May 2026 shows: Job PJ-2026-0015 issued 52.3g of gold, recovered 49.8g, waste 2.5g (4.8%). The Casting stage had the highest loss at 1.2g. This helps management negotiate with workers on reducing casting waste.
9. User Management

Who can access: Administrator only.

Adding a New User
1
Click Administration → User Management in the sidebar.
2
Click New User.
3
Fill in:
  • Username — used for login (no spaces)
  • First Name / Last Name
  • Email
  • Employee ID — your internal HR code
  • Department — e.g. "Casting", "Polishing", "Admin"
  • Role — choose from the 6 roles above
  • Password — the user can change this after first login via their profile
4
Click Create User. Share the username and password with the employee securely.
Deactivating a User

To remove access without deleting the account: edit the user and uncheck Active. The user cannot log in, but all their historical records (process records, QC checks) are preserved.

Security: Never share the admin account. Create individual accounts for each employee so the system can correctly attribute work and timestamps to the right person.
